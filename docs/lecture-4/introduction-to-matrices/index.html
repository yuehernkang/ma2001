Introduction to Matrices (Part 1)

Definition of Matrix​

A matrix (plural: matrices) is a rectangular array of numbers.

(a11a12…a1na21a22…a2n⋮⋮⋮⋮am1am2…amn)\begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1n}\\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2n}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots\\ a_{m1} & a_{m2} & \dots & a_{mn}\\ \end{pmatrix}⎝⎜⎜⎜⎜⎛​a11​a21​⋮am1​​a12​a22​⋮am2​​……⋮…​a1n​a2n​⋮amn​​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎟⎞​

  • mmm is the number of rows in the matrix
  • nnn is the number of columns in the matrix
  • The size of the matrix is given by m∗nm * nm∗n
  • The (i,j)(i,j)(i,j)-entry is the entry in the iiith row & jjjth column.
  • In the given matrix, the (i,j)(i,j)(i,j)-entry is aija_{ij}aij​

Example​

  • (12340−1)\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2\\ 3 & 4\\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}⎝⎛​130​24−1​⎠⎞​ is a 3∗23 * 23∗2 matrix (3 rows, 2 columns)

  • The (1,2)(1,2)(1,2) entry is 222 and the (3,1)(3,1)(3,1)-entry is 000.

  • (23.1−231200π0)\begin{pmatrix} \sqrt{2} & 3.1 & -2\\ 3 & \frac{1}{2} & 0\\ 0 & \pi & 0 \end{pmatrix}⎝⎛​2​30​3.121​π​−200​⎠⎞​ is a 3∗33 * 33∗3 matrix

  • (210)\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}(2​1​0​) is a 1∗31 * 31∗3 matrix

  • (112)\begin{pmatrix} 1\\ 1\\ 2\\ \end{pmatrix}⎝⎛​112​⎠⎞​ is a 3∗13 * 13∗1 matrix

  • (4)\begin{pmatrix} 4 \end{pmatrix}(4​) is a 1∗11 * 11∗1 matrix, usually treated as a real number in computation. For instance, this matrix is equivalent to 444.

  • Empty matrix does not exist in this module

A matrix is usually denoted by capital letters A,B,C,…A,B,C,\dotsA,B,C,…

  • m∗nm * nm∗n matrix AAA = (a11a12…a1na21a22…a2n⋮⋮⋮⋮am1am2…amn)\begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1n}\\ a_{21} & a_{22} & \dots & a_{2n}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots\\ a_{m1} & a_{m2} & \dots & a_{mn}\\ \end{pmatrix}⎝⎜⎜⎜⎜⎛​a11​a21​⋮am1​​a12​a22​⋮am2​​……⋮…​a1n​a2n​⋮amn​​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎟⎞​

Special Matrices​

A row matrix (row vector) is a matrix with only one row.

  • (210)\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}(2​1​0​) is a row matrix (row vector)

A column matrix (column vector) is a matrix with only one column

  • 112\begin{aligned} 1 \\ 1\\ 2\\ \end{aligned}112​ is a column matrix (column vector)

A square matrix is a matrix with the same number of rows and columns.

  • An n∗nn * nn∗n matrix is called a square matrix of order nnn
  • (816357492)\begin{pmatrix} 8 & 1 & 6\\ 3 & 5 & 7\\ 4 & 9 & 2\\ \end{pmatrix}⎝⎛​834​159​672​⎠⎞​ is a square matrix of order 333.
  • This is a 3∗33*33∗3 matrix

Square (Diagonal) Matrix​

A square matrix is called a diagonal matrix if all its non-diagonal entries are zero.

A=(a11a12…a1na21a22…a2n⋮⋮⋱⋮an1an2…ann)A = \begin{pmatrix} \colorbox{blue}a_{11} & a_{12} & \dots & a_{1n}\\ a_{21} & \colorbox{blue}a_{22} & \dots & a_{2n}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ a_{n1} & a_{n2} & \dots & \colorbox{blue}a_{nn}\\ \end{pmatrix}A=⎝⎜⎜⎜⎜⎛​a​11​a21​⋮an1​​a12​a​22​⋮an2​​……⋱…​a1n​a2n​⋮a​nn​​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎟⎞​ A=(aij)n∗nA=(a_{ij})_{n*n}A=(aij​)n∗n​ is diagonal ⇔aij=0\hArr a_{ij}=0⇔aij​=0 for all i≠ji \not ={j}i=j.

Example​

  • (0001)\begin{pmatrix} \colorbox{blue}0 & 0 \\ 0 & \colorbox{blue}1\\ \end{pmatrix}(0​0​01​​)
  • As long as all the non-diagonal entries are zero, then it is a diagonal matrix, regardless whether the diagonal entries are zero or not.
  • (a000b000c)\begin{pmatrix} \colorbox{blue}a & 0 & 0\\ 0 & \colorbox{blue}b & 0\\ 0 & 0 & \colorbox{blue}c\\ \end{pmatrix}⎝⎛​a​00​0b​0​00c​​⎠⎞​, where a,b,ca,b,ca,b,c are (possibly zero) numbers.
