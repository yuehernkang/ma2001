Elementary Matrices

Elementary Operations​

Recall the elementary row operations of matrices.

  1. Multiply a row by a nonzero constant
  2. Interchange two rows
  3. Add a constant multiple of a row to another row

Using Elementary Matrices​

An elementary matrix is a matrix which differes from the identity matrix by ONE single elementary row operation.

It is able to generate the different elementary row operations (interchange, add a row to another, multiply by a constant).

Types of Elementary Row Operations​

Switching Rows with an Elementary Matrix​

Given the identity matrix I=(100010001)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1\\ \end{pmatrix}I=⎝⎛​100​010​001​⎠⎞​

This will result in A=AIA=AIA=AI

If we want to switch the rows of AAA using an elementary matrix, we just swap the rows of the identity matrix.

For example, we want to swap row 1 and row 2.

Therefore, we will swap row 1 and row 2 of the identity matrix, giving us an elementary matrix

E1=(010100001)E_1 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0\\ 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1\\ \end{pmatrix}E1​=⎝⎛​010​100​001​⎠⎞​

What we will do is pre-multiplication, so we put the elementary row matrix that we constructed E1E_1E1​ at the front of the matrix we want to perform operations on.

Example:

A=(123456789)A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3\\ 4 & 5 & 6\\ 7 & 8 & 9\\ \end{pmatrix}A=⎝⎛​147​258​369​⎠⎞​

As we can observe, the rows of 111 and 222 has been swapped.

Multiplying a row by a scalar​

Multiplying a row by a scalar is very straight forward.

Given the identity matrix I=(100010001)I = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1\\ \end{pmatrix}I=⎝⎛​100​010​001​⎠⎞​

and A=(123456789)A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3\\ 4 & 5 & 6\\ 7 & 8 & 9\\ \end{pmatrix}A=⎝⎛​147​258​369​⎠⎞​

Let's say we want to obtain 5R25R_25R2​ of matrix AAA

We just multiply row 222 of the identity matrix by 555.

We just perform the same row operation to the identity matrix.

We will get :

E2=(100050001)E_2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 5 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 1\\ \end{pmatrix}E2​=⎝⎛​100​050​001​⎠⎞​

Let B=(abcdef)B= \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ c & d\\ e & f\\ \end{pmatrix}B=⎝⎛​ace​bdf​⎠⎞​

Then, we apply pre-multiplication of the elementary matrix to the matrix we want to perform operations on.

E2B=(ab5c5def)E_{2}B = \begin{pmatrix} a & b\\ 5c & 5d\\ e & f\\ \end{pmatrix}E2​B=⎝⎛​a5ce​b5df​⎠⎞​
