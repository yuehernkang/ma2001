Transpose Of Matrix

Let A=(aij)m×nA = (a_{ij})_{m\times n}A=(aij​)m×n​ be a matrix

  • The transpose of AAA is the n×mn \times mn×m matrix ATA^TAT or (AtA^tAt)
    • whose (i,j)(i,j)(i,j)-entry is ajia_{ji}aji​

Example​

Let A=(12345678)A=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 & 4\\ 5 & 6 & 7 & 8\\ \end{pmatrix}A=(15​26​37​48​)

AT=(15263748)A^T = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 5\\ 2 & 6\\ 3 & 7\\ 4 & 8\\ \end{pmatrix}AT=⎝⎜⎜⎜⎛​1234​5678​⎠⎟⎟⎟⎞​ and (AT)T=(12345678)(A^T)^T = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 & 4\\ 5 & 6 & 7 & 8\\ \end{pmatrix}(AT)T=(15​26​37​48​)

Remarks:

  • The iiith row of ATA^TAT is the iiith column of AAA.
  • The jjjth column of ATA^TAT is the jjjth row of AAA.

Properties of Matrix Transpose​

Theorem​

Let AAA be an m×nm \times nm×n matrix

  • (AT)T=A(A^T)^T = A(AT)T=A
  • AAA is symmetric ⇔A=AT\hArr A = A^T⇔A=AT
  • Let ccc be a scalar. Then (cA)T=cAT(cA)^T = cA^T(cA)T=cAT
  • Let BBB be m×nm \times nm×n. Then (A+B)T=AT+BT(A+B)^T = A^T + B^T(A+B)T=AT+BT
  • Let BBB be m×pm \times pm×p. Then (AB)T=BTAT(AB)^T = B^TA^T(AB)T=BTAT

Proof of (AB)T=BTAT(AB)^T = B^TA^T(AB)T=BTAT​

  • LHS:
    • ABABAB is m×p⇒(AB)Tm \times p \rArr (AB)^Tm×p⇒(AB)T is p×mp \times mp×m
  • RHS:
    • BTB^TBT is p×np \times np×n & ATA^TAT is n×m⇒BTATn \times m \rArr B^TA^Tn×m⇒BTAT is p×mp \times mp×m.

So (AB)T(AB)^T(AB)T and BTATB^TA^TBTAT have the same size.
